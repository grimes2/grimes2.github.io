Sumerian Heptachords

(pdf) Babylonian Tonal System: Heptachords and Scales

7 scales

The Sumerian and Babylonian Tonal System is based on consecutive heptachords. In the mode kitmum higher pitches of the scale show flatwards shift, lower pitches sharpwards shift.

The scale of kitmum is unique. The basic heptachord is palindromic. The center note is D. The scale is related to pītum and qablītum and is in a kind of center position.

10 semitones per heptachord.

7 tones per heptachord, 6 unique tones.

7 heptachords are shown, but 6♯ = 6♭. So there are only 6 unique heptachords. This can be compared to the days of a week and the role of the sunday.

In total 60 semitones. The number 60 (sexagesimal) is assoziated with the highest Sumerian god An, representing the sky. The scale is cyclic like a watch.

The scales pītum and qablītum build also a pair. The key signatures of the scales are identical. Center notes are E and C, difference is a major third.

The ambitus is 5 octaves. 5x1200=6000 Cent or 6x1000=6000 Cent, 60 semitones, 200 Cent per whole tone, 100 Cent per semitone, proposed equal temperament (Plato: corresponding to celestial city Callipolis). In Sumerian music only just intonation was known with whole tone 204 Cent and semitone 90 Cent. An alternative just intonation was found in Nippur. The delta of the key signature is 2. The scale has 36 unique notes. Babylonian astronomers developed a 36 stars system.

The scales išartum and embūbum are related. The center of the symmetrical scales are different. Center note (Balance point) of išartum is G, center of embūbum is A. The difference is a heptachord. The center note can be interpreted as the main finalis (f). So the Babylonian Tonal System probably knows already the concept of finalis in constructing a melody.

The preceding 5 scales all have two semitones and 4 whole tones in a heptachord. 6 heptachords build a scale, two scales are possible. The 12 heptachords address the 12 notes of the complete tone circle.

The last two scales nīd qablim and nīš tuḫrim are very different from the first 5 scales. Center notes are B and F. The basic heptachord contains only one semitone. Tritones exist. The delta of the key signature is 5. 3 heptachords exist with 7x3=21-2=19 pitches. In Islam, Maryam, مريم is the 19th sūrah of the Qur'an, which announces Jesus. In Baháʼí Faith, a group of 19 is called a Váhid, which means unity. In total 33 semitones, not cyclic.
